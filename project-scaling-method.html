Revisiting the X-ray Scaling Method with MCMC

Background

Outbursts in Black Hole X-ray Transients (BHXTs) are characterized by spectral state transitions, which reflect variations in the mass accretion rate. These transitions form the basis of the X-ray Scaling Method, used for black hole mass estimation. However, scaling parameters often differ between the rise and decay phases, introducing challenges for precise measurements.

Methods

We performed spectral fitting and scaling curve analysis on several BHXT systems, separating rise and decay phases. The initial parameter estimates were obtained using scipy.optimize.curve_fit, followed by Bayesian inference through Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling with emcee. The parameter distributions were evaluated using scipy.stats, with uncertainties visualized via corner plots.

Results

The study revealed clear differences in scaling parameters between rise and decay phases:

  • The decay phase showed a steeper photon index slope compared to the rise phase.
  • Total radiated energy in the decay phase was about twice as high as in the rise phase.
  • These differences highlight the need to account for phase-dependent physical processes in black hole mass estimation.

Python Libraries Used

  • numpy, pandas – data cleaning and preprocessing
  • matplotlib, scienceplots – scientific visualization
  • scipy.optimize.curve_fit – initial parameter fitting
  • emcee – MCMC sampling for posterior distributions
  • scipy.stats – statistical evaluation of parameter distributions
  • corner – visualization of multidimensional parameter posteriors
