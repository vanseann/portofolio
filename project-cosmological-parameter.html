Cosmological Parameter Estimation

Background

Determining cosmological parameters is fundamental to understanding the large-scale structure and evolution of the Universe. In the standard ΛCDM model, key parameters such as the matter density (Ωm), dark energy density (ΩΛ), and the Hubble constant (H0) define the dynamics of cosmic expansion and structure formation.

The observational data used in this project originates from the work of Astier et al. (2008), which combines two main sets of Type Ia supernova observations: data from The Supernova Legacy Survey (SNLS) and a compilation of nearby supernova data from Hamuy et al. (1996), Riess et al. (1999), Krisciunas et al. (2001), Jha (2002), Altavilla et al. (2004), Krisciunas et al. (2004a), Krisciunas et al. (2004b), and Strolger et al. (2002). These combined datasets provide a robust foundation for constraining cosmological parameters.

Methods

Using observational data including Type Ia supernovae and Hubble parameter measurements, the model predictions from ΛCDM cosmology were fit to the data. Parameter estimation was first attempted with scipy.optimize.curve_fit, followed by a Bayesian approach using Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) with emcee. Posterior distributions were analyzed with corner plots to extract parameter constraints.

Results

  • Constraints on H0 are consistent with recent Planck and SH0ES results within uncertainties.
  • The matter density parameter Ωm is well constrained, while ΩΛ dominates at late cosmic times.
  • MCMC results provide credible intervals for each parameter, improving robustness compared to simple curve fitting.
